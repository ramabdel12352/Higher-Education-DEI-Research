This project analyzes the impact of recent legislative changes on diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) efforts across higher education institutions in Texas, with a specific focus on the Dallas–Fort Worth (DFW) region. It combines institutional research, policy analysis, and real-world reporting to evaluate how Senate Bill 17 (SB 17) is reshaping university structures, student support systems, and campus climate.

The dataset and findings include a comprehensive review of major public and private universities in the DFW area, documenting whether DEI offices exist, have been modified, or have been dismantled following the implementation of SB 17. The research highlights how public institutions—such as the University of North Texas—have already begun eliminating DEI offices, while others are restructuring programs to remain compliant with state law while attempting to preserve inclusion efforts.

Beyond institutional data, this project synthesizes key policy developments and expert perspectives from academic leaders, legal scholars, student organizations, and policymakers. It explores both sides of the debate: supporters argue that removing DEI offices promotes merit-based systems and reduces ideological bias. At the same time, critics emphasize the loss of critical support systems for underrepresented students and potential threats to academic freedom.

The analysis also examines the broader consequences of these policy changes, including:

Reduced diversity-focused recruitment efforts
Cuts to mentorship and support programs for marginalized students
Elimination of DEI staff positions
Increased legal and administrative uncertainty for universities
Potential long-term decline in campus diversity and inclusion

Additionally, the project highlights the growing tension between state-level political decisions and institutional autonomy in higher education. It situates Texas within a broader national trend in which DEI programs are increasingly scrutinized, debated, and, in some cases, restricted.

Overall, this repository serves as both a research resource and a policy analysis tool for understanding how legislative actions directly impact higher education systems, student experiences, and institutional priorities. It is especially useful for students, researchers, policymakers, and anyone interested in education policy, equity in higher education, and the evolving role of DEI initiatives in the United States.
